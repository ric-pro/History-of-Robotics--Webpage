What is Robotics?
The multidisciplinary field of robotics encompasses engineering, science, and the design, build, usage, and maintenance of robots. These devices are designed to carry out jobs that have historically been completed by people, frequently more accurately and effectively.
Background of Robotics
The field of robotics encompasses the study, development, use, and maintenance of robotic systems. Robots are programmable machines that carry out tasks and interact with the real environment. They range from basic automated devices to intricate artificial intelligence-driven systems. The history of robots has its origins in the ancient world. During the industrial revolution, humans developed the structural engineering capability to control electricity so that machines could be powered with small motors. In the early 20th century, the notion of a humanoid machine was developed. The first uses of modern robots were in factories as industrial robots. These industrial robots were fixed machines capable of manufacturing tasks which allowed production with less human work. Digitally programmed industrial robots with artificial intelligence have been built since the 2000s.
Brief history of Robotics
The history of robotics is a testament to human ingenuity and creativity. It spans thousands of years, starting from the ancient times when humans first dreamed of artificial helpers, to the modern era where robots are an integral part of our daily lives. On this website, we delve into the early history of robotics, exploring ancient automata and the concept of artificial helpers in ancient civilizations. We then transition into the birth of modern robotics in the 20th century, highlighting key inventions and the people behind them.
