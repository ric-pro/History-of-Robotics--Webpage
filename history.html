Three Major Achievements from Each Decade Over the Last Century is given in the table below:
Decade Major Incident
1920s
  1. 1921: The term "robot" was coined by Czech playwright Karel ÄŒapek in his play "R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots)
  2. 1926: Westinghouse introduced "Televox," one of the earliest examples of a remotely controlled robot.
  3. 1928: Elmer and Elsie, the first humanoid robots, were created by Captain W.H. Richards and A.H. Reffelt.
1930s
  1. 1937: Alan Turing publishes his paper "On Computable Numbers," laying the groundwork for modern computing and its relationship to robotics.
  2. 1939:"Elektro," the first humanoid robot capable of speech, was unveiled at the 1939 New York World's Fair.
  3. 1939: William Grey Walter developed the "tortoises," autonomous robots that could respond to light and touch stimuli.
1940s
  1. 1948: Norbert Wiener published "Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine," introducing the concept of cybernetics and its influence on robotics.
  2. 1948: The first digitally operated and programmable robot, "The Unimate," was created by George Devol and Joseph Engelberger.
  3. 1949: Claude Shannon published "Programming a Computer for Playing Chess," a groundbreaking paper in the field of artificial intelligence and robotics.
1950s
  1. 1954: George Devol filed the first patent for a programmable manipulator, leading to the development of industrial robotics.
  2. 1956: John McCarthy coined the term "artificial intelligence" and organized the Dartmouth Conference, marking the birth of AI as a field of study closely related to robotics.
  3. 11959: Shakey the Robot, developed at Stanford Research Institute, became one of the first mobile robots to use AI techniques for decision-making and navigation.
1960s
  1. 1961: The first industrial robot, the Unimate, went into operation at a General Motors plant in New Jersey, revolutionizing manufacturing processes.
  2. 1967: The Stanford Arm, a robotic manipulator, was developed at Stanford University, laying the groundwork for future robotic arms used in various industries.
  3. 1969: The first walking robot, the "WABOT-1," was developed by Ichiro Kato at Waseda University in Japan.
1970s
  1. 1973: The "Puma" series of industrial robots, developed by Victor Scheinman, became widely used in manufacturing due to their versatility and reliability.
  2. 1979: Hiroshi Makino created the first microcomputer-controlled electric industrial robot, further advancing automation in manufacturing.
  3. 1978: Stanford's Cart, a mobile robot, successfully navigated its way through obstacles in an unstructured environment, demonstrating advancements in AI and robotics.
1980s
  1. 1983: The development of the first robotic surgical system, the "Arthrobot," marked a significant advancement in medical robotics.
  2. 1986: Honda unveiled "P2," its second humanoid robot, showcasing advancements in bipedal locomotion and balance control.
  3. 1989: Hans Moravec published "Mind Children," proposing the concept of "mind uploading" and exploring the future of AI and robotics.
1990s
  1. 1997: IBM's Deep Blue defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov, demonstrating the capabilities of AI in strategic decision-making..
  2. 1997: NASA's Mars Pathfinder mission successfully deployed the Sojourner rover, showcasing robotic exploration capabilities on another planet.
  3. 1998: Sony introduced the AIBO robotic dog, showcasing advancements in consumer robotics and artificial intelligence.
2000s
  1. 2002: Honda introduced ASIMO, a humanoid robot capable of advanced mobility and interaction with humans.
  2. 2004: Boston Dynamics introduced the BigDog quadruped robot, showcasing advancements in robotic mobility and terrain traversal.
  3. 2007: The DARPA Urban Challenge showcased autonomous vehicle technology, with several robotic cars successfully navigating an urban environment.
2010s
  1. 2011: IBM's Watson won the game show Jeopardy!, demonstrating advancements in natural language processing and AI.
  2. 2013: Google acquired several robotics companies, including Boston Dynamics, signaling a significant investment in robotics technology.
  3. 2016: SoftBank introduced Pepper, a humanoid robot designed for social interaction and assistance in various settings.
2020s
  1. 2020: The COVID-19 pandemic accelerates interest in robotics for tasks such as disinfection, telepresence, and contactless delivery.
  2. 2021: Tesla's Full Self-Driving (FSD) Beta rollout showcases advancements in autonomous vehicle technology, although concerns remain about safety and regulatory approval.
  3. 2022: Boston Dynamics' Spot robot becomes increasingly adopted for various applications, including inspections, security, and entertainment.
